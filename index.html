Introduction

The purpose of this exploratory data analysis was to take a different approach to bring awareness of the important of climate change, air pollution and the impact it has on our health. According to the National Geographic Society, climate change is the long-term alteration of temperature and typical weather patterns in a place. This definition mentions the aspect of changes in weather patterns, but the climate change subject can be more complex than that.

My research focusses specificality in analyzing air pollution indicators. A 99% of the world population is exposed to bad quality air, and most of us are not even aware of how the quality of air is measured. My goal is to explain the basic of the Air Quality Index and its relationship with climate change to educate an audience that might be interested in learning more about this subject.

About The Data

To conduct this exploratory data analysis I used multiple datasets, but the primary was the World Development Indicators dataset from The World Bank Data Catalog. I used this dataset because The World Bank's mission is committed to reducing poverty and rising to the challenges of climate change. Also, to collect this data, The World Bank works with well recognized international sources, and many important organizations worldwide relay on their research and expertise.
