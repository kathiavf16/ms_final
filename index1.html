Plane crashes are a recurring nightmare for Russia, which earned the dubious distinction from the Aviation Safety Network of being the deadliest place to fly in 2011.
      Why, according to the most recently available data, is flying in Russia four times deadlier than the world average? In large part, the air industry here is a victim of its own success: As the number of passengers has skyrocketed in recent years, airlines in Russia have struggled to find enough qualified personnel. The result, experts have said, is more under-trained, inexperienced and overconfident pilots.      Source: Russia's Air Industry Is a Victim of Its Own Success


Top 20 Plane Operators Involved in Accidents

                  Top 20 Operators involved in Accidents Sorted by Number of Fatalities






As Russia has been involved in an alarming number of accidents, it is not a surprise that Aeroflot has the highest number of fatalities. According to the Aircraft Crashes Record Office, 8,231 passengers have died in Aeroflot crashes, about five times more than any other airline. From 1946 to 1989, the carrier was involved in 721 incidents. However, from 1995 to 2017, the carrier was involved in 10 incidents.

The US Air Force is the following with 3,392 fatalities. According to the Air Force Times, Air Force mishaps rose 16 percent between 2013 and 2017, the years following the congressional budget cuts known as sequestration. At least 133 service members were killed in those fiscal years 2013-2017 mishaps, according to data obtained by Military Times. Source: Air Force aviation accidents reach seven-year high as low-level mishaps soar


      Fatalities Over Time - 1972 The Deadliest Year


In 1972 there was a record of 2,937 fatalities making it the deadliest year in this dataset. According to the article "The year of flying dangerously: 1972", There was 72 airliner crashes in 1972 (defined by the Aviation Safety Network as hull loss of a 14 seat or greater aircraft). Crashes were happening for age-old reasons, chiefly controlled flight into terrain (CFIT) and loss of control in flight (LOC), but to new and larger aircraft. Source: The year of flying dangerously: 1972


Aeroflot was involved in 524 or 17% of the total of fatalities.
